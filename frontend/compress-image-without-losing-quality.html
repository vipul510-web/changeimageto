Frequently Asked Questions

Is it really possible to compress without losing quality?

While some quality loss is inevitable with lossy compression, at High Quality (90) setting, the difference is imperceptible to the human eye while achieving significant file size reduction (20-40%).

What's the best quality setting?

High Quality (90) provides minimal quality loss, while Balanced (85) offers the best balance between file size reduction and quality preservation for most uses.

Is this tool free to use?

Yes, our tool is completely free with no signup required and no watermarks.

How much can I compress without losing quality?

At High Quality (90), you can typically reduce file size by 20-40% with imperceptible quality loss. At Balanced (85), you can reduce by 40-60% with minimal quality loss.

What format preserves quality best?

For compression with quality preservation, JPG at 90 quality offers excellent results. WEBP at 90 quality can provide even better compression with similar quality.
