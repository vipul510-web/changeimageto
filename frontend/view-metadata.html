How to View Image Metadata

  1. Upload your image or video file using the uploader above.
  2. Click View Metadata to extract all embedded information.
  3. Review the metadata displayed, including EXIF data, camera settings, GPS coordinates, and more.
  4. Use the information to understand your file's properties or identify privacy concerns.

What Metadata Can You View?

  • Camera Information: Make, model, lens details, serial number
  • Photo Settings: ISO, aperture (f-stop), shutter speed, focal length, flash settings
  • Date & Time: When the photo was taken, created, or modified
  • Location Data: GPS coordinates, altitude, direction (if available)
  • Image Properties: Dimensions, resolution, color space, orientation
  • Copyright: Photographer name, copyright notices, usage rights
  • Software: Editing software used, version information

Why View Image Metadata?

  • Privacy Check: See if your photos contain GPS coordinates or location data before sharing
  • Photo Organization: Use date and camera information to organize your photo library
  • Learning: Understand camera settings used in professional photos
  • Verification: Check if images have been edited or modified
  • Copyright Protection: Verify copyright information in your images

Frequently Asked Questions

What is image metadata?

Image metadata, also called EXIF data, is hidden information embedded in image files. It includes camera settings, date/time, GPS coordinates, and other technical details about how the photo was taken.

Can I view metadata from any image?

Most images contain some metadata, but the amount varies. Photos taken with smartphones or digital cameras typically have extensive EXIF data, while edited or converted images may have less metadata.

Does viewing metadata modify my image?

No, viewing metadata is a read-only operation. Your original image file remains completely unchanged.

Can I see GPS coordinates in metadata?

Yes, if your image contains location data, you'll see GPS coordinates, altitude, and direction information. This is especially common in photos taken with smartphones that have location services enabled.

Is the metadata viewer free to use?

Yes, our metadata viewer is completely free. No registration, no watermarks, and no hidden fees.
