What is Bokeh Effect?

Bokeh (pronounced "boh-keh") is a Japanese term meaning "blur" or "haze." In photography, it refers to the aesthetic quality of the out-of-focus areas in an image, typically characterized by soft, circular light spots and smooth background blur.

Bokeh effects are created by blurring backgrounds while keeping the subject sharp, resulting in a professional, dreamy look that's popular in portrait photography, product photos, and social media content.

Bokeh vs Regular Blur

While both create blurred backgrounds, bokeh specifically refers to the aesthetic quality of the blur - soft, smooth, and often with circular light spots. Our Blur Background tool creates bokeh-like effects by using advanced blur algorithms that mimic professional camera lenses.

Regular blur simply makes backgrounds out of focus, while bokeh creates an artistic, dreamy effect that's visually pleasing and professional-looking.
